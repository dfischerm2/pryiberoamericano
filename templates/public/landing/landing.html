IX Ibero-American Biometrics Meeting 2025

The State University of Milagro (UNEMI), in collaboration with the Spanish Council of Ibero-American Studies and the University of Salamanca, invites academics, researchers, and professionals to the IX Ibero-American Biometrics Meeting (EIB) 2025 in Quito, Ecuador. This international congress will address advances in biometrics and statistics in areas such as health, agriculture, and sustainability, fostering collaboration and innovation. Join us to connect with experts, share knowledge, and promote scientific and sustainable progress in Ibero-America.

About
About Us

Learn about this event

The IX Ibero-American Biometrics Meeting is an international academic congress that has brought together experts since 2007. Originally organized by the University of Salamanca and the Spanish Council of Ibero-American Studies, this event promotes the exchange of knowledge in statistical and mathematical methods applied to disciplines such as Biology, Medicine, Psychology, Pharmacology, and Agriculture. Known for its scientific rigor and interdisciplinary collaboration, the meeting fosters dialogue among Ibero-American researchers and the dissemination of biometric and statistical advances to drive scientific development.

Topics to be Developed

  • Biometric methods for epidemiological analysis
  • Application of statistical models in personalized medicine
  • Biometrics for public health policy evaluation
  • Use of biometrics to measure and improve human wellbeing

  • Biometric analysis for natural resource management
  • Applied statistics for climate change mitigation and adaptation
  • Biometric tools in sustainable agriculture and food security
  • Mathematical models for environmental and energy impact analysis

  • Innovations in statistical and computational methods
  • Big data models and machine learning
  • Advanced experimental design and multivariate analysis
  • Use of emerging software and technologies

  • Strengthening international research networks
  • Collaborative projects between academia, public and private sectors
  • Impact on higher education and professional training

  • Statistical methods to address social inequalities
  • Use of biometrics in migration and demographic studies
  • Statistics and data analysis in security and social justice
